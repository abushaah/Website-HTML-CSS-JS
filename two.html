The Court

Participants in the court



The Judge
  • Makes decisions on the admissibility of evidence.
  • Controls events in the courtroom.
  • Interprets the law.
  • Instructs the jury on points of law.
 The Witness
  • Give evidence concerning their knowledge of the circumstances surrounding a crime.
  • Perjury is knowingly making false statements in court while giving evidence.
The Defence
  • The Accused or Defendant is the person charged with a crime.
  • The Defence Counsel is the lawyer who represents the accused.
  • If the accused pleads not guilty, the Defence tries to show a “reasonable doubt” exists.
  • If the accused pleads guilty or is found guilty, the Defence recommends an appropriate sentence.
 The Prosecution or Crown
  • The Prosecutor or Crown Counsel is the lawyer representing the government’s interest in investigating and punishing criminal offences.
  • Presents credible evidence that must show the accused is guilty “beyond a reasonable doubt”.
The Jury
  • A group of 12 men and women, chosen by the Crown and Defence Counsel from a pool of ordinary citizens.
  • Listen to arguments, examine the evidence, follow judge’s instructions about the law.
  • For eligibility, the juror must be a Canadian citizen, 18 years of age or older, and a resident of the province for at least one year.
  • A juror is ineligible if they are a politician or work in the justice system.
  • A potential juror may be exempt for religious reasons, if they show financial hardship (since jury duty is not paid), or if they have served on a jury within the past two years.
 Other Court Personnel
  • Court Clerk
    • Keeps a record of trial exhibits, administers oaths, announces the beginning and end of the court session.
  • Court Reporter
    • Records verbatim, everything that has been said during the trial (one of the most stressful jobs in the world!).
  • The Public
    • Public cases welcome anyone who wants to watch what happens in their courtrooms.
